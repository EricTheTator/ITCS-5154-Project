Good afternoon,

The Post-9/11 GI Bill generally offers better benefits, including full tuition coverage (up to in-state rates), a housing allowance (BAH), and a book stipend.

The Montgomery GI Bill provides a fixed monthly stipend for tuition but does not include BAH.
Switching to the Post-9/11 GI Bill could be a better option if you need more support.

Hi Military & Veteran Services,
As a club leader, it’s no secret how difficult it can be to create a sense of real friendship and connection among the members of your club. I know this because I had the same experience with my clubs. We were always looking for unique, fun ways for our members to connect, so a few years ago we started a business offering Paint & Sip pottery events on campuses across the US to do just that. We create a laid-back, creative space on campus where everyone gets to make something unique and experience each other outside of the club setting.
Club leaders have told us how these events helped them blow right past the initial stiffness and awkwardness that comes with new club members as it allows people to get to know each other in a special way that a normal club meeting could never really provide. Groups like the Association for Students of Color and Medium Design Collective loved it, and I know Military & Veteran Services will too.
We bring all the materials, handle the setup, and then give you all the space to enjoy the time in your own way. Some groups make it a themed event catered to their club's focus, while others will keep it simple. The event runs about 1.5 hours, and we can accommodate your schedule to find the right time for you all.
It's always easiest to connect face to face, so you can find a time on my calendar here (https://calendly.com/steph-stonemeetsclay/20min) to learn more about the process and ask any questions you may have.
Can’t wait to see what your club creates!

<p style="margin: 0pt  0pt 0pt 0pt;"
>My fellow North Carolinians,&nbsp;
</P>
<p style="margin: 0pt  0pt 0pt 0pt;">&nbsp;
</P>
<p style="margin: 0pt  0pt 0pt 0pt;">The destruction caused by Helene is incomprehensible and has left many communities in Western North Carolina absolutely devastated. The last thing that the victims of Helene need right now is political posturing, finger-pointing, or conspiracy theories that only hurt the response effort. The immediate focus needs to be supporting search and rescue operations and ensuring the safety of everyone in harm&#8217;s way.
</P>
<p style="margin: 0pt  0pt 0pt 0pt;"
>&nbsp;
</P>
<p style="margin: 0pt  0pt 0pt 0pt;">Make no mistake that the full recovery process will be long and difficult. So many families have lost their homes and all their belongings, while entire communities have been destroyed by flooding. I will do everything in my power to help make them whole again, and I have already started working in the U.S. Senate to pursue long-term federal assistance for families and communities left devastated from the flooding.<br>
<br
>This long-term federal assistance will be the key to Western North Carolina&#8217;s recovery over the coming months and years to rebuild homes, infrastructure, farms, roads, and highways that were damaged or destroyed. I will also keep pushing to change federal law to get rid of unnecessary red-tape and&nbsp;bureaucracy that needlessly delays the start of the long-term rebuilding process.&nbsp;<br>
<br
>When it comes to immediate needs for federal assistance, please know there are a number of programs available for homeowners, small business owners, and farmers who have suffered property damage or loss. If you have a question about the process or are not getting the help you need from the federal government, please don&#8217;t hesitate to contact my office and we will assist you. My office number is <strong>(704) 509-9087.</strong
>
</P>
<p style="margin: 0pt  0pt 0pt 0pt;">
